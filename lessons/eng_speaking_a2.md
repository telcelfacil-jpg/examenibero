# Oxford Test of English: Speaking

The Speaking section checks if you can answer simple personal questions, describe a picture and produce short connected sentences. At A2 level, clarity is more important than complexity.

## What you need to do

- answer basic questions about yourself,
- describe what you can see,
- compare or explain simple ideas,
- speak in short but complete sentences.

## Useful structure for A2

When you describe a picture, use this order:

1. **Place:** "This is in a park / classroom / kitchen."
2. **People:** "There are two children."
3. **Actions:** "They are playing / studying / eating."
4. **Extra detail:** "They look happy because..."

## Sample answer frame

"In this picture I can see a family in a kitchen. The mother is cooking and the children are helping. They look happy. I think they are preparing dinner."

That answer is simple, but it is complete and well organized.

## Common mistakes

- saying only isolated words,
- forgetting the verb,
- repeating "there is" without adding action,
- stopping too early.

## Final advice

Do not try to sound advanced. Try to sound clear, organized and calm. A short complete answer is stronger than many broken words.
