# 🇬🇧 Phase 1: Present Simple vs. Present Continuous (Academic English Foundations)

## Mastering Your Daily Habits and Your Present Actions

To succeed in the Oxford placement exam (Level A2) for the Ibero Secondary School, you must be able to distinguish clearly between things that happen **regularly** and things that are happening **right at this very second**. In English, we use two different drumbeats (tenses) for these situations.

### 1. The Present Simple: The Habit Maker

We use this tense for routines, habits, facts, and permanent states. It is the tense of "Always" and "Never".

- **Structure:** Subject + Verb (in its base form) + Complement.
- **The "Special S":** For *He, She*, and *It*, you **MUST** add an "s" or "es" to the verb. (Example: *I study*, but *She studies*).
- **Key Words:** always, usually, often, sometimes, every day, on Mondays.

*Example:* "I always eat breakfast at 7 AM." (Routine).

---

## Master Strategy: The "State" Verb Rule

Native speakers have a secret: verbs of "feeling" or "thinking" (like love, hate, want, know, understand) are **almost never** used in the Continuous form.

- **Correct:** *I understand the lesson.*
- **Incorrect:** *I am understanding the lesson.*

---

### 2. The Present Continuous: The Action Hero

We use this for actions happening **now**, at the exact moment of speaking, or for temporary situations.

- **Structure:** Subject + Verb TO BE (am/is/are) + Verb ending in -ING + Complement.
- **Key Words:** now, right now, at the moment, today, this week.

*Example:* "I am reading this guide right now." (Current action).

---

## 3. Dueling Tenses: Present Simple vs. Continuous

Imagine Ariadna's profile:

- **Routine:** "Ariadna **studies** every afternoon." (Simple)
- **Today:** "But today, she **is visiting** her grandmother." (Continuous)

The Ibero exam will try to trick you by mixing these in the same paragraph. Look for the "Time Markers" (the key words mentioned above) to know which one to pick.

### Common Errors to Avoid

1. Forgetting the "am/is/are" in the Continuous. (Wrong: *I studying*).
2. Forgetting the "s" for He/She/It in the Simple. (Wrong: *He study*).
3. Using Continuous for habits. (Wrong: *I am eating pizza every Friday*).
