# Oxford Test of English: Listening

The Listening section checks if you can understand spoken English in common situations such as school, transport, shopping or daily routines. At A2 level, you do not need to catch every single word. You need to catch the important information.

## What you may hear

- short conversations,
- public announcements,
- simple instructions,
- people talking about plans or routines.

## What the test wants from you

- identify who is speaking,
- understand place, time or purpose,
- notice a correction,
- catch the final decision.

## Listening strategy

### 1. Read the question before the audio

This helps your brain know what to look for. If the question asks for time, listen for hours or moments of the day. If it asks for place, listen for names of locations.

### 2. Listen for key information

Focus on names, times, numbers, days and changes.

Example:

"The train to Oxford leaves from platform 4, not platform 2."

The important word is the correction: **not platform 2**. The answer is **platform 4**.

### 3. Be careful with distractors

Sometimes speakers mention one option first and then change it.

Example:

"Let's meet on Friday... actually, Saturday is better."

The answer is **Saturday**, not Friday.

### 4. Stay calm if you miss one word

A missed word does not destroy the whole audio. Keep listening for the next clue.

## Mini guided example

Audio script:

"Hi, Emma. The English club starts at five today because the teacher has a meeting at four."

Question: What time does the English club start?

- A) At four
- B) At five
- C) At six

Correct answer: **B**.

The number four appears, but it refers to the teacher's meeting, not the club.

## Useful expressions to recognise

- **at the moment**
- **after school**
- **next to**
- **on Saturday**
- **half past**
- **quarter to**

## Final advice

Listening improves when you predict, listen for the correction and ignore small mistakes. Your mission is to catch the message, not to translate every sentence perfectly.
